Red Velvet Cake

Description

This is a cake to stop traffic. The layers are an improbable red that can vary from flourescent pink to a dark ruddy mahogany.

The color, often enhanced by buckets of food coloring, becomes even more eye-catching against clouds of snowy icing.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 3.5 cups cake flour
  • .5 cup unsweeted cocoa (not Dutch process)
  • 1.5 teaspoons salt
  • 2 cups canola oil
  • 2.25 cups granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 6 tablespoons (3 ounces) red food coloring
  • 1.5 teaspoons vanilla
  • 1.25 cup buttermilk
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 2.5 teaspoons white vinegar

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Place teaspoon of butter in each of 3 round 9-inch layer cake pans and place pans in oven for a few minutes until butter melts. Remove pans from oven, brush interior and sides of each with butter and line bottoms with parchment.
  2. Whisk cake flour, cocoa, and salt in a bowl.
  3. Place oil and sugar in bowl of an electric mixer and beat at medium speed until well blended. Beat in eggs one at a time. With machine on low, very slowly add red food coloring. (Take care; it may splash). Add vanilla. Add flour mixture alternately with buttermilk in two batches. Scrape down bowl and beat just long enough to combine.
  4. Place baking soda in a small dish, stir in vinegar and add to batter with machine running. Beat for 10 seconds.
  5. Divide batter among pans, place in oven and bake until a cake tester comes out clean, 40 to 45 minutes. Let cool in pans 20 minutes. Then remove from pans, flip layers over and peel off parchment. Cool completely before frosting.
